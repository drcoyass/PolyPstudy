Molecular Odyssey

History of Intelligence

Tracing the evolution of polyphosphate from discovery to the future.

1890

Discovery of Polyanions

L. Liebermann first characterized metaphosphates in yeast, opening the door to the study of high-energy inorganic polymers.

1956

The Kornberg Milestone

Nobel laureate Arthur Kornberg identified Polyphosphate Kinase (PPK), proving that polyphosphate is biologically active and enzymatically regulated.

2004

Mammalian Integration

Discovery of significant polyphosphate stores in human platelets and mitochondria, redefining its role in blood coagulation and energy metabolism.

FUTURE

Regenerative Frontiers

Modern applications in mucosal barrier strengthening (COVID-19 protection) and bone regeneration mark the beginning of a new era in molecular medicine.
